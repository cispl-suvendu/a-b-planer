# Problem Statement

## Purpose

This document defines the core problem that Landing Page A/B Planner exists to solve.

It establishes the product's reason for existence and serves as the primary filter for future product decisions.

If a proposed feature does not directly help solve one or more of the problems documented here, it should be deferred or rejected.

---

# The Core Problem

Most businesses understand that improving their website's conversion rate can significantly increase revenue.

However, they struggle with a much simpler question:

> **"What should we test first?"**

The challenge is rarely a lack of tools.

The challenge is a lack of direction.

Businesses have analytics platforms, heatmaps, A/B testing frameworks, and AI assistants, yet they still don't know which experiments are most likely to improve conversions.

---

# The Current Reality

A typical optimization workflow looks like this:

Launch website

↓

Acquire traffic

↓

Notice poor conversion rates

↓

Open analytics tools

↓

Collect lots of data

↓

Discuss ideas internally

↓

Receive conflicting opinions

↓

Delay experimentation

↓

Make random changes

↓

Hope conversion rates improve

This process is slow, inconsistent, and heavily dependent on experience.

---

# Why Existing Solutions Fall Short

Most existing tools solve only part of the problem.

### Analytics Platforms

Provide data but rarely recommend what to test.

Users still need to interpret the results.

---

### Heatmaps & Session Recordings

Reveal user behavior but not the highest-impact experiments.

They answer **what happened**, not **what should be done next**.

---

### A/B Testing Platforms

Help teams run experiments after ideas already exist.

They provide infrastructure, not strategy.

---

### General AI Assistants

Can generate suggestions, but outputs are often:

- Generic
- Inconsistent
- Poorly structured
- Difficult to prioritize
- Lacking business context

Users must still organize, validate, and evaluate the recommendations.

---

# Customer Pain Points

Our users commonly experience:

- Uncertainty about what to optimize
- Too many ideas with no prioritization
- Limited CRO expertise
- Limited engineering resources
- Pressure to improve results quickly
- High advertising costs with poor conversion rates
- Inconsistent optimization processes

---

# Cost of the Problem

When businesses fail to prioritize experimentation effectively, they often experience:

### Financial Cost

- Wasted advertising spend
- Lower revenue
- Reduced marketing ROI
- Lost opportunities

---

### Operational Cost

- Slow experimentation
- Long planning cycles
- Inefficient meetings
- Repeated discussions

---

### Strategic Cost

- Decisions based on opinions
- Delayed product improvements
- Reduced competitive advantage
- Missed growth opportunities

---

# Root Causes

The problem exists because:

- CRO expertise is expensive.
- Teams lack structured experimentation frameworks.
- Existing tools assume users already know what to test.
- AI assistants lack specialization.
- Recommendations are rarely prioritized.
- Business context is often ignored.

---

# Our Solution

Landing Page A/B Planner provides structured, AI-powered experimentation planning.

Instead of overwhelming users with raw analysis, the platform delivers:

- Prioritized A/B test hypotheses
- Clear reasoning
- Suggested variants
- Implementation guidance
- Estimated impact
- Confidence scores
- Difficulty estimates
- Business-focused recommendations

The result is a practical roadmap rather than a collection of disconnected ideas.

---

# What We Are Not Solving (MVP)

To remain focused, the MVP intentionally does **not** solve:

- Running A/B tests
- Traffic analytics
- Heatmaps
- Session recordings
- User surveys
- SEO optimization
- Marketing automation
- Continuous website monitoring
- Multi-page optimization
- Team collaboration
- Report exporting

These capabilities may be considered in future phases but are outside the scope of Version 1.

---

# Product Principles Derived from the Problem

Every recommendation should:

- Reduce uncertainty.
- Save time.
- Prioritize actions.
- Be easy to understand.
- Be easy to implement.
- Explain the reasoning.
- Focus on measurable business outcomes.

---

# Success Indicators

We know we are solving the problem when users:

- Know exactly what to test next.
- Stop relying on guesswork.
- Implement recommendations with confidence.
- Complete analyses in minutes instead of hours.
- Return to the platform for future optimization work.

---

# Product Decision Filter

Before approving a feature, ask:

1. Does it help users identify better experiments?
2. Does it reduce uncertainty?
3. Does it save meaningful time?
4. Does it improve recommendation quality?
5. Does it increase trust in the platform?
6. Does it strengthen our competitive advantage?

If the answer is "No" to most questions, the feature should be reconsidered.

---

# AI Guidance

When generating recommendations:

- Focus on practical business outcomes.
- Prioritize recommendations by expected impact.
- Explain why each recommendation matters.
- Avoid generic advice.
- Present information in a structured and actionable format.

The AI should behave like an experienced CRO consultant who understands both marketing and product strategy.
