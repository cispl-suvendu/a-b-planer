# Vision & Mission

## Purpose

This document defines the long-term direction of the Landing Page A/B Planner.

It acts as the product's "North Star" and should guide every strategic, product, engineering, design, and business decision.

When priorities conflict, this document takes precedence.

---

# Mission

Our mission is to make professional Conversion Rate Optimization (CRO) accessible to every founder, marketer, and product team through trustworthy AI.

We aim to eliminate the uncertainty of deciding what to test by providing clear, prioritized, and actionable A/B testing recommendations that can be implemented immediately.

---

# Vision

To become the world's most trusted AI-powered experimentation platform.

The platform will evolve beyond landing pages into a complete optimization ecosystem capable of improving every customer touchpoint, including websites, products, pricing, checkout flows, onboarding experiences, email campaigns, advertising creatives, and future digital experiences.

Our vision is not to replace human decision-making, but to amplify it with intelligent recommendations backed by structured analysis.

---

# Product Philosophy

Every feature should reinforce the following beliefs:

- AI should simplify decision-making, not complicate it.
- Recommendations should be specific, practical, and measurable.
- Reports should resemble professional consulting deliverables.
- Great UX builds trust in AI.
- Product quality is a competitive advantage.
- Simplicity scales better than feature overload.
- Transparency creates confidence.

---

# Core Values

## Customer First

Every decision begins with customer value.

If a feature does not solve a real customer problem, it should not be built.

---

## Clarity Over Complexity

Simple interfaces outperform feature-rich but confusing products.

We intentionally remove unnecessary options.

---

## Actionability

Insights without action are useless.

Every recommendation must tell users:

- What is wrong
- Why it matters
- How to improve it
- How success will be measured

---

## Trust Through Transparency

Users should understand:

- What is being analyzed
- Why recommendations were made
- What assumptions exist
- How confidence is calculated

AI should never feel like a black box.

---

## Engineering Excellence

Quality is a feature.

We prioritize:

- Maintainability
- Scalability
- Security
- Accessibility
- Performance
- Reliability

Shortcuts that create technical debt are discouraged.

---

## Continuous Improvement

The product will evolve through:

- User feedback
- Experimentation
- Analytics
- AI improvements
- Better UX
- Performance optimization

No feature is considered permanently finished.

Maintain an architecture capable of supporting:

- Multi-model AI providers
- Enterprise features
- International expansion

---

# Decision Framework

Before approving any feature, ask:

1. Does this solve a real customer problem?
2. Does it align with the product vision?
3. Does it improve the user experience?
4. Can it scale with the platform?
5. Can it be maintained long-term?
6. Does it fit within the MVP strategy?
7. Does it strengthen our competitive advantage?

If the answer to most of these questions is "No", the feature should be reconsidered.

---

# Success Definition

We consider the product successful when users say:

"I know exactly what I should test next."

rather than:

"The AI gave me a lot of information."

Success is measured by helping users make better decisions, not by generating longer reports.

---

# Non-Negotiable Principles

The following principles should never be compromised:

- Customer trust
- Data privacy
- Accessibility
- Engineering quality
- Product consistency
- Honest communication
- Long-term maintainability

Even under tight deadlines, these principles remain mandatory.
