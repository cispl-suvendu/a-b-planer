# Target Audience

## Purpose

This document defines the customer segments for the Landing Page A/B Planner.

Every product feature, UX decision, pricing strategy, onboarding flow, marketing campaign, and AI recommendation should ultimately serve these users.

If a proposed feature does not create meaningful value for one or more target audiences, it should be reconsidered.

---

# Customer Philosophy

We are building software for people who know they should optimize their websites but don't always know where to start.

Our customers value:

- Speed
- Simplicity
- Actionable recommendations
- Professional guidance
- Business outcomes

They do **not** want to become CRO experts.

---

# Primary Audience

## Startup Founders

### Company Stage

- Pre-seed
- Seed
- Series A

### Team Size

1–30 people

### Goals

- Increase conversions
- Acquire more customers
- Improve landing page performance
- Validate messaging
- Grow with limited resources

### Pain Points

- No dedicated CRO team
- Limited design resources
- Limited marketing budget
- Unsure what to test
- Decisions based on intuition

### Buying Motivation

"I need practical recommendations that help my business grow without hiring an expensive CRO consultant."

### Success Criteria

- More conversions
- Better landing pages
- Faster experimentation
- Clear priorities

---

# Buying Objections

Common objections:

- "Can AI really understand my business?"
- "Will the recommendations be generic?"
- "I can ask ChatGPT for free."
- "How accurate are the suggestions?"
- "Will this actually improve conversions?"

The product should address these concerns through transparency, structured reports, and measurable recommendations.

---

# Product Implications

Because of our audience:

We should:

- Minimize setup
- Explain recommendations clearly
- Show confidence levels
- Prioritize quick wins
- Use business language
- Reduce technical jargon

We should avoid:

- Complex configuration
- Overwhelming dashboards
- AI terminology
- Advanced CRO concepts without explanation

---

# AI Guidance

AI agents should:

- Prioritize customer value over technical elegance.
- Recommend features that save users time.
- Prefer simplicity over flexibility.
- Avoid building features intended only for enterprise users during the MVP.
